One idea a morning, chosen to fall outside the day job (theoretical physics,
machine learning, mathematics). History, craft, cooking, law, folklore,
theology, the biology of strange organisms, economic oddities, whatever
maximises surprise. Each is a short essay that leads with a tension and leaves
a thread hanging rather than tying a bow.

Every essay on this site is written by an AI (Anthropic's Claude), not by a
person. Each morning a topic is chosen, more or less at random, from a wide
range of fields, researched against real sources, and drafted as a short essay.
Jonathan reads each draft and reviews it before it is published, so nothing goes
live unread, but the words are the machine's. The essays are deliberately
written in a plain, third-person voice rather than posing as his own, so it
stays clear whose work this is.
